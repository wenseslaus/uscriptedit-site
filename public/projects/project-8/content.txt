Music Connect Africa.

 This is a regional programme led by British council aimed at strengthening creative and cultural industry based in the music sector in Sub Saharan Africa. The goal is to suppport economic and social development focusing on the youth and under represented communities. By combining industry experts, artistic development and cross cultural collaboration, MCA creates a platform music sector proffesional to engage organically, meaningful with local and international networks.   

 MCA in Tanzania is implemented by Ongala Music through an hybrid approach which involves training, workshops, creative collaboration and live performance opportunities. The programme nutures music sector proffesionals while amplifying availability, diversity and commerecial viability. 

As a art and culture manager I was part of the program whereby.
 
 1. I developed as  music curator, where I got to interact with facilitators who are experienced and upskilled my knowledge in history of Tanzania music and technical skills to assist artists in developing the skills and presenting a perfectly curated performance to the audience. 

 2. I developed skills around brand & identity, IP Copyrights and Legal. And in relation with creatives on the program i used the skills to enahnce the quality of the artist and beyond. 

 3. As a moderator I got to control intergenerational talks between artists, culture contributors and the community about the importance of artist development and authenticity in art.


This experience to interact and work with artists and producers by collaborating in the artistic process to develop their skills and also curate art that is valuable and impactful allowed me to bridge the creative process and music business as an artist manager.
