The Nest at the Climate Justice Camp 2024.

As an artist and curator, I was part of the ArtCrew that co-created The Nest, a participatory art and wellbeing space within the Climate Justice Camp in Arusha, Tanzania.

The Nest served as an alternative, open environment—separate from the camp's structured program—where participants could rest, reflect, and express themselves freely. It was designed as a “third space”: not a workshop or formal discussion setting, but a living, evolving space rooted in creativity, care, and connection.

I contributed to the setup and activation of the space, helping shape both its physical and emotional atmosphere. Through collaborative making and spatial design, we transformed a simple outdoor area into an immersive environment that invited participation, curiosity, and stillness.

My practice within The Nest centered on art as a healing and reflective tool. I supported the creation of a space where participants could process complex emotions around climate justice through drawing, making, and play. This approach encouraged non-verbal expression and allowed individuals to engage with their experiences in a more intuitive and personal way.

As a curator, I helped foster an inclusive and non-hierarchical environment where people could share ideas, experiment creatively, and connect beyond the expectations of formal sessions. The space became a site for spontaneous conversations, collective artworks, and moments of vulnerability—offering participants an alternative way to engage with both the camp and each other.

The Nest ultimately evolved into a shared, home-like environment, one that held space for both individual reflection and collective expression, reinforcing art's role in healing, storytelling, and community building.
