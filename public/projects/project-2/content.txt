Mzizi ni Msingi: A Millenial's Perspective.

Introduction: The Creative Spark

As long as I can remember, I have always been an art and music enthusiast. To me, the creative scene was fascinating, and I always wanted to understand how all these things came to life. As a kid, my energy was vibrant and channeled toward curiosity. Whenever I engaged in any artistic activity, I felt peace and tranquility. Growing up in the late 1990s and early 2000s, Tanzania was coming of age in the information and communication sectors, hence there was a lot to consume compared to past eras.

The earliest vivid memories I have are of the beats, rhythms, and rhymes from different genres that could be heard and felt at home, my friends’ places, and all around the neighborhood. Then there were the blockbuster films from Hollywood and Bollywood, screened at the local cinema we called Bijampora — “Bijas” for short.

Any time there were parties, such as weddings and Holy Communion, as kids we would get immersed in the vibe and come alive dancing. I can still feel the rush to this moment, reminiscing about Sebene, Taarab, Bongo Fleva, or Congolese Rhumba and Champeta. The music scene was one hundred percent media-controlled, as different radio shows and programs influenced the consumption of music as a product.
