Disability Empowerment Through Art(DETA).

The DETA Project (Disability Empowerment Through Art) is an initiative under the Nipa Foundation aimed at enhancing the creative, social, and economic well-being of marginalized communities, particularly people living with disabilities. The project focuses on the Pwani region (Kisarawe, Kibaha, Bagamoyo) and seeks to empower participants through art-based skill development, mental health support, and community engagement.
Project Goals
The primary objectives of the DETA Project are:
To equip 20 individuals with disabilities with practical art skills such as beadwork, batik, and soap making.
To provide a platform for mental health support and counseling.
To foster economic independence and social inclusion through creative expression.
To create a supportive community where participants can share their stories and talents.

Conclusion and Overall Impact
The DETA Project has made a profound impact by empowering participants not only with practical skills but also with confidence and a sense of belonging. By creating a space for creative expression and community support, the project has contributed to social inclusion and has paved the way for a more inclusive society.
Beyond the direct training and support, the project also benefited from partnerships with various CSOs and BASATA, who provided crucial advice and sustainability alternatives. This collaborative effort ensured that the platform remains not only accessible but also inclusive, paving the way for a more equitable art scene and broader inclusion of marginalized communities

The DETA Project is a continuous project which started with a three-month initiative aimed at empowering under-represented communities, particularly people with disabilities, by providing them with practical skills, confidence, and a sense of belonging. Based in Kisarawe, Kibaha, and Bagamoyo district, the project focused on nurturing participants through artistic creation and design, fostering both social and economic growth.

As a curator, I was responsible for designing interactive events that brought together  artists as trainers and trainees as participants with disabilities, creating an environment of collaboration, learning, and feedback. We also created a platform for trainees to share their personal stories , highlighting their journeys and aspirations. The showcase allowed participants to communicate the significance of inclusion and foster greater social cohesion and economic development.
In practice, the DETA Project was a transformative initiative that bridged gaps and empowered marginalized communities, enriching the broader arts community and society in general.
